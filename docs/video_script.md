They'd find a supplier on Alibaba, send money for samples that looked great — then the bulk order would show up with completely different quality. Or they'd get ghosted after payment. Or they'd have no idea if the factory was even real.

The problem isn't that good factories don't exist. They do. The problem is that buyers have no way to tell who's legit — from 10,000 kilometers away.

That's why I started SilkBridge.

I'm based right here in the Pearl River Delta — Guangdong province — which is basically the manufacturing capital of the world. Within two hours of where I'm sitting, there are industrial clusters for knives and tools in Yangjiang, precision CNC parts in Dongguan, aluminum and building hardware in Foshan, and garments in Guangzhou.

I know these factories. I've been inside them. I speak their language — literally and figuratively.
